## Purpose

This document defines the Dad Mimic entity layer in Kaegan.exe.

The Dad Mimic is not a normal enemy.

The Dad Mimic is an escalation layer.

It exists to corrupt the idea of safety.

Dad's room should feel like the place Kaegan wants to reach.

The Dad Mimic makes that desire dangerous.

## Core Rule

The Dad Mimic should begin as sound and feeling before it becomes visual.

The sound is first interpreted as ordinary house noise. It should be subtle and should not be overdone.

The player should hear it before they see anything.

The player should feel it in the house before they understand what it is.

The Dad Mimic should not be physically revealed until the end of the game, after the final note is found.

## In-Game Naming Rule

The Dad Mimic must never be directly named inside the game.

The name belongs in development documentation only, just like Bad Dream Mommy.

The player should not read a note that says "Dad Mimic."

Kaegan should not understand the Dad Mimic as an entity.

Kaegan has never met the person the mimic is based on.

The Dad Mimic is connected to Kaegan's grandfather, not Kaegan's father.

Because Kaegan has no direct context for his grandfather, the player should first assume the sounds are part of the house or another expression of Bad Dream Mommy.

The player should experience the Dad Mimic through sound, rhythm, dread, and corrupted safety before understanding that it is something separate.

## Final Note Reveal Setup

After Kaegan finds the final note, the birthday note from his real dad, the Dad Mimic finally reveals itself.

The reveal happens when the player lowers the note.

The player should hear crutches rushing toward them.

That sound causes the player to lower the note.

The Dad Mimic is then revealed as a deformed entity.

This should be sudden, brief, and terrifying.

## Emotional Role

The Dad Mimic corrupts comfort.

Bad Dream Mommy makes the house feel unsafe.

The Dad Mimic makes safety feel unsafe.

Dad's room should represent rescue, comfort, familiarity, or the place Kaegan wants to reach.

The Dad Mimic should slowly turn that signal into dread.

The Dad Mimic should not be interpreted as Kaegan's father.

The Dad Mimic is connected to Kaegan's grandfather, whom Kaegan has never met.

The player should become afraid of the thing behind the safety signal after Bad Dream Mommy has already been established and after the house has denied the route to Dad's room.

## When The Dad Mimic Begins

The Dad Mimic should not appear at the very beginning.

The player should first learn:

- the house controls movement
- Bad Dream Mommy controls thresholds
- notes and drawings matter
- the bedroom is not permanently safe
- hallways cannot be trusted

After the first false waking loop, the Dad Mimic layer can begin.

The player should already understand the house before this new layer appears.

## Sound-First Design

The Dad Mimic begins through house sounds.

Important sounds:

- crutch clinks
- prosthetic leg clicks
- weighted steps
- slow patient walking
- wood or floor pressure
- pauses between movements
- rhythm changing from natural to unnatural

The sound should start believable.

At first, it should sound like someone walking with crutches or a prosthetic leg.

Then the rhythm should become wrong.

## Rhythm Rule

The Dad Mimic's rhythm is the main scare.

At first, the rhythm should sound natural and human.

Then it should slowly change.

The timing can become:

- too slow
- too long between steps
- too patient
- too heavy
- too close
- too deliberate
- too uneven
- too impossible

The player should feel the rhythm become less human before anything is shown.

## Example Sound Feeling

The Dad Mimic sound should feel like:

```text
clink... clunk

clink... clunk

clink...... clunk

clink............... clunk
```

The spacing matters.

The silence between sounds should become part of the fear.

The player should wait for the next sound.

The waiting should feel worse each time.

## Relationship To The House

The Dad Mimic should feel like the house is using a memory incorrectly.

It should not feel like a normal ghost pretending to be Dad.

It should feel like the house remembered the sound of Dad but not the meaning of Dad.

The house understands the shape of comfort.

The house does not understand love.

That is what makes the mimic wrong.

## Relationship To Bad Dream Mommy

Bad Dream Mommy owns the doorway fear.

The Dad Mimic owns the safety corruption fear.

Bad Dream Mommy should feel immediate, visual, and threshold-based.

The Dad Mimic should feel distant, patient, and sound-based.

Bad Dream Mommy makes the player afraid to look at doors.

The Dad Mimic makes the player afraid to reach Dad's room.

They should not behave the same way.

## Visual Restraint

The Dad Mimic should not be seen early.

The player may see signs of it before seeing it directly.

Possible signs:

- shadows at the end of a hallway
- crutch marks
- a door moving slightly
- a note appearing near Dad's room
- a sound coming from behind a closed door
- a distorted silhouette too far away to understand

The Dad Mimic should remain mostly unseen until the final reveal.

## Final Reveal Rule

The Dad Mimic should be physically revealed only at the end.

This reveal should happen after Kaegan reaches the final note.

The reveal should be sudden, earned, and brief.

It should not become a boss fight.

It should not start a combat sequence.

It should be the final jump scare.

After the final jump scare, Kaegan wakes in his bed.

It is daytime.

The game ends.

## What The Dad Mimic Should Not Do

Do not reveal the Dad Mimic too early.

Do not make the Dad Mimic chase the player like a normal monster.

Do not make the Dad Mimic speak too much.

Do not explain what it is.

Do not use the name "Dad Mimic" in-game.

Do not make it goofy or exaggerated.

Do not make it a combat enemy.

Do not let the player defeat it.

The Dad Mimic is not there to be beaten.

It is there to corrupt the safest signal in the house.

## Gameplay Use

The Dad Mimic can be used to:

- redirect the player away from Dad's room
- make the player hesitate before entering a hallway
- make silence feel dangerous
- signal that a false wake has changed the house
- escalate the nightmare after Bad Dream Mommy is established
- guide the player toward the final note
- make the final reveal feel inevitable

## First Build Use

The first playable build does not need the Dad Mimic fully implemented.

For the first build, the Dad Mimic can be represented only through sound placeholders or not included yet.

If included early, it should only be a distant sound hint.

The first build should prioritize:

- opening bedroom sequence
- dishes crash
- carpet-drag footsteps
- door-face event
- first drawing
- long hallway
- turn-around note
- bedroom door slam

The Dad Mimic belongs to later escalation.

## Guiding Statement

The Dad Mimic is frightening because it sounds like safety before it becomes wrong.

It should not rush.

It should not explain itself.

It should wait.

It should walk with patience.

It should make the player afraid of reaching the place they wanted to reach.
