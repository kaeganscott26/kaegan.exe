## Purpose

This document records the remembered house layout for Kaegan.exe.

The game does not need to recreate the house with perfect real estate accuracy.

The game should recreate the emotional logic of the house.

The house layout should preserve important routes, thresholds, rooms, and memory points.

The playable version may exaggerate distance, stretch hallways, block routes, and change room behavior for horror.

This floor plan is not an exact architectural blueprint.

It is a memory floor plan used to preserve the emotional layout, major room relationships, and playable horror routes.

The map only needs to feel close enough to the remembered house.

It does not need to be perfect because Kaegan never lived in the real house.

The floor plan should support the game logic, not trap the design.

## Core Layout Rule

The game uses a memory version of the house.

The layout may be distorted by the nightmare.

Hallways may stretch.

Rooms may appear farther away than they were.

Doors may lead incorrectly.

Familiar paths may end in walls.

But the emotional relationships between spaces should remain recognizable.

## Major Areas

The house can be understood as several connected zones:

- old bedroom / bathroom / laundry junction
- kitchen
- Dad's office
- dining room
- living room
- hallway to the three-room section
- middle room and bathroom
- Dad's room
- east-end room where Kaegan wakes

## Old Bedroom Area

The user's old room was at the other end of the house.

This is not the room Kaegan wakes up in during the opening sequence.

This room connects to a small junction space near the bathroom, laundry room, and kitchen route.

From the old room area:

- bathroom is nearby
- laundry room and garage route are to the left when leaving
- going straight leads toward the kitchen

This room can be used later as a memory room or secondary route after the house opens up.

## Bathroom Near Old Room

The bathroom near the old room is part of the old bedroom zone.

This area helps establish the real house layout and gives the player a believable domestic space.

Bathrooms should feel unsafe because they contain mirrors, narrow angles, and partial visibility.

## Laundry Room / Garage Route

The laundry room is to the left when leaving the old room area.

The laundry room connects toward the garage.

This route can imply escape.

The house should control whether this route is available.

The laundry / garage path can be used as a denied or delayed escape route.

## Kitchen

The kitchen is reached by going straight from the old room junction.

The kitchen is important because the first major horror sound comes from this side of the house.

The dishes crash should originate from the kitchen or kitchen-side of the house.

The kitchen has tile flooring, wood cabinets, counters, and strong domestic familiarity.

This space should feel ordinary before the nightmare changes it.

The kitchen connects toward:

- the old room / laundry / bathroom junction
- dining room
- living room
- Dad's office

## Dad's Office

Dad's office is reached from the kitchen side.

The room at the end of the kitchen is Dad's office.

This room is part of the safety signal because it is connected to Dad.

The house may use Dad's office as a destination, blocked path, or later escalation route.

Dad's office should not be treated as a random room.

It carries emotional weight.

## Dining Room

The dining room connects the kitchen and living room area.

One reference angle is from the doorway to Dad's office while facing west.

From this angle, the dining room shows the route back toward the old room side of the house.

The dining room can work as a transition space between kitchen safety and living room openness.

## Living Room

The living room is a larger open space.

It can be entered from the hallway doorway that leads toward the three-room section.

The living room has a fireplace, built-in shelving, wood paneling, carpet, and large windows or sliding glass doors.

Because the living room is open, it should not automatically feel safe.

The openness can make the player feel exposed.

The living room connects toward:

- kitchen / dining side
- hallway to the three-room section
- other open family areas

## Hallway To Three-Room Section

This hallway is one of the most important spaces in the game.

The hallway leads toward the three rooms.

At the east end is the room where Kaegan wakes up.

Dad's room is also connected to this hallway.

There is a middle room and bathroom between the end room and Dad's room.

This is the hallway Kaegan goes down in the opening sequence.

In the real memory layout, the hallway should lead toward Dad's room.

In the nightmare version, the hallway should deny that destination.

The player runs down the hallway expecting to reach Dad's room.

Instead of reaching Dad's room, the hallway ends in a wall.

This denial is one of the first major impossible-space events.

## East-End Room / Kaegan Wake Room

The room at the east end of the hallway is the room where Kaegan wakes up in the game.

This is not the user's old room.

This room becomes the opening bedroom.

The player starts here in bed.

At first, the player cannot move.

The player can only look around.

This room should feel like a normal bedroom before the house begins performing the nightmare.

The room should include:

- low light
- visible bedroom door
- closet or wall features
- ordinary carpet and trim
- enough visibility to feel trapped

The opening sequence begins here.

## Dad's Room

Dad's room is one of the scariest rooms in the house.

The doorway and room geometry are important.

Dad's bed was positioned on a wall facing the bathroom area.

Because of this angle, someone or something could enter the room and not be visible until it cleared the wall.

This made the doorway feel dangerous.

The player should not be able to fully trust the entrance to Dad's room.

Dad's room should represent safety, but its geometry should make safety feel uncertain.

This room should be used carefully.

It is not just another bedroom.

It is an emotional destination.

## Middle Room And Bathroom

Between the east-end room and Dad's room are a middle room and bathroom.

The middle room is connected to mimic memory.

This is the room where the mimic was heard.

The bathroom and middle room help make the hallway feel segmented.

They create places where the player expects movement, sound, or partial visibility.

The house can use these rooms to hide sound sources or misdirect the player.

## Opening Route

The opening route should follow this emotional structure:

- Kaegan wakes in the east-end room
- movement is locked
- dishes crash comes from the kitchen side of the house
- footsteps approach the bedroom door
- Bad Dream Mommy appears through or at the door
- movement unlocks
- player collects the first drawing
- bedroom door opens
- player enters the hallway
- player tries to reach Dad's room
- hallway stretches or feels wrong
- Dad's room is denied
- hallway ends in a wall
- note says "Turn around"
- player returns toward the starting bedroom
- bedroom door slams shut
- house opens another path

## Important Horror Geometry

The house should use real layout fear.

Important geometry includes:

- long hallway sightlines
- doors at the end of halls
- bathroom corners
- rooms where someone can enter unseen
- doorways that hide what is around the wall
- kitchen sounds traveling through the house
- open living room exposure
- laundry / garage route suggesting escape

## Memory Accuracy vs Game Design

The game should not be trapped by perfect accuracy.

The house should feel emotionally accurate first.

If a real route needs to be longer, darker, blocked, or shifted for horror, that is allowed.

The goal is not to make a real estate replica.

The goal is to make the player feel the remembered fear of the house.

The things that need to be perfect are the fear rules, sound timing, doorway behavior, hallway denial, Dad Mimic rhythm, final note, and final scare.

The map only needs to be close enough to support those moments.

## Guiding Statement

The house should feel ordinary enough to be real and wrong enough to be remembered as a nightmare.

The player should feel that every room has a normal purpose.

Then the house should slowly change what those purposes mean.
