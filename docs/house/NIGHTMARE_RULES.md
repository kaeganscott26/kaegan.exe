## Purpose

This document defines the core nightmare rules for Kaegan.exe.

These rules control how the house behaves across the entire game.

The game should not feel like a normal haunted house with random scares.

The game should feel like a childhood nightmare remembered too clearly.

The house should behave with intention.

The player should feel like the house is guiding, blocking, watching, and redirecting them.

## Prime Rule

The house performs the story.

The house should not explain itself directly.

The house uses:

- sound
- darkness
- silence
- door behavior
- notes
- drawings
- movement restriction
- impossible space
- false waking
- familiar routes becoming wrong

The house is the main threat.

## No Combat Rule

There is no combat in Kaegan.exe.

The player does not fight Bad Dream Mommy.

The player does not fight the Dad Mimic.

The player does not attack the house.

The horror comes from helplessness, dread, exploration, memory, sound, and environmental control.

Combat would break the emotional logic of the game.

## No Death Rule

The player should not traditionally die.

When the nightmare catches Kaegan, the result should usually be a reset, false waking, route change, or escalation.

The player should feel trapped in a loop, not sent to a game-over screen.

Failure should mean:

- waking back up
- losing access to a path
- hearing a new sound
- seeing a changed room
- triggering a new layer of the nightmare
- being forced to continue

The nightmare does not end the game.

The nightmare changes the game.

## Movement Control Rule

The house can take control away from the player.

The game may restrict:

- walking
- sprinting
- turning
- interaction
- door use
- escape routes

Movement restriction should be used intentionally, not constantly.

The player should feel the loss of control when it matters.

The strongest movement states are:

- look-only paralysis
- slow movement during pursuit
- locked door denial
- forced route redirection
- sprint appearing only when the house allows it

## Look-Only Rule

Look-only sequences should be used for helpless nightmare moments.

During look-only sequences, the player can move the camera but cannot move the body.

This should be used when the house wants the player to witness something.

Look-only should feel like sleep paralysis, not like a cutscene.

The player should still feel present.

## Doorway Rule

Doorways are not just transitions.

Doorways are weapons.

Doorways can:

- show Bad Dream Mommy
- hide Bad Dream Mommy
- block the player
- open without permission
- refuse to open
- lead somewhere wrong
- mark a threshold between safety and danger
- become unsafe before the room does

The player should never fully trust a doorway.

## Drawing And Note Rule

Notes and drawings are progression keys and haunting triggers.

They are not optional collectibles in the first build.

They should:

- unlock doors
- trigger sounds
- change routes
- reveal story fragments
- prove that an event happened
- show that the house remembers
- guide the player without direct tutorial text

Notes and drawings should never over-explain the whole story.

They should give context, not answers.

## Sound Rule

Sound is one of the main languages of the house.

The house should speak through sound before it speaks through visuals.

Important sound types:

- dishes crashing
- carpet-drag footsteps
- SHHFFFT doorway movement
- doors shutting
- distant house movement
- crutch clinks
- prosthetic leg clicks
- heavy patient walking
- silence after loud events

Silence is also a sound design tool.

After a loud event, silence should make the player wait.

## Silence Rule

Silence should not feel empty.

Silence should feel like the house is listening.

Silence should be used after:

- dishes crashing
- footsteps
- door movement
- visual appearances
- false waking
- route changes

The player should learn to fear quiet moments.

## Impossible Space Rule

The house does not have to obey normal layout rules.

Hallways can stretch.

Doors can lead to the wrong place.

Rooms can move.

A familiar route can become blocked.

A safe path can become unsafe.

The house should not become random.

Impossible space should feel intentional.

The player should feel like the house is choosing where they are allowed to go.

## False Wake Rule

False waking is a major nightmare structure.

When Kaegan is almost caught or overwhelmed, he may wake back up in the bedroom.

This should feel like relief for only a moment.

Then the player should realize something has changed.

False waking can change:

- available doors
- sounds in the house
- note locations
- hallway length
- lighting
- Bad Dream Mommy behavior
- Dad Mimic presence

A false wake is not a restart.

A false wake is an escalation.

## Bad Dream Mommy Rule

Bad Dream Mommy should never be directly named inside the game.

She should behave as a threshold presence first.

She is strongest when she is:

- partially visible
- still
- wide-eyed
- hard to understand
- leaning without entering
- tied to doorways
- accompanied by SHHFFFT movement sounds

She should not become a normal enemy.

She should not be overused.

The player should fear the possibility of her before seeing her clearly.

## Dad Mimic Rule

The Dad Mimic should begin as sound and feeling before becoming visual.

The Dad Mimic should corrupt the idea of safety.

Dad's room should feel like a place Kaegan wants to reach.

The house should make that path unstable.

The Dad Mimic begins through:

- crutches
- prosthetic leg clicks
- clunks
- patient weighted walking
- rhythms that start natural and become unnatural

The Dad Mimic should not be physically revealed until the end.

The final reveal should feel earned.

## House Memory Rule

The house remembers.

The house should repeat sounds, shapes, drawings, notes, and routes in changed forms.

A repeated event should not be exactly the same every time.

It should feel familiar enough to be recognized and changed enough to be feared.

The house should feel like it is replaying a memory incorrectly.

## Player Guidance Rule

The player should rarely be told what to do directly.

If the house wants the player to move, it should:

- change light
- open a door
- close a door
- place a note
- place a drawing
- repeat a sound
- deny a familiar path
- make one route feel worse than another

The house should guide through behavior.

## Escalation Rule

The game should escalate by changing rules, not by simply getting louder.

Escalation can include:

- longer hallways
- slower movement
- repeated sounds closer to the player
- new locked doors
- drawings appearing in impossible places
- Bad Dream Mommy appearing in new threshold forms
- Dad Mimic sounds entering the house
- false waking changing the environment

The game should not rely on constant jump scares.

## Restraint Rule

The scariest version of the game shows less than the player wants to see.

Do not reveal everything.

Do not explain everything.

Do not show the full monster too early.

Do not make every sound loud.

Do not make every room dangerous.

Fear needs quiet space around it.

## First Build Rule

The first build only needs to prove the nightmare logic.

It does not need final art.

It does not need final models.

It does not need full animation.

It does not need full house layout.

It must prove:

- movement can be locked and unlocked
- sound events can trigger
- a doorway scare can happen
- drawings or notes can act as keys
- a hallway can deny the expected route
- the bedroom can stop being safe
- the house can force the player deeper

## Guiding Statement

Kaegan.exe should feel like a nightmare with rules.

The rules should be felt before they are understood.

The player should learn that the house controls safety, direction, sound, memory, and movement.

The house does not chase the player.

The house decides what chasing means.
